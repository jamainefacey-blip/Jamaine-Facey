Travelling alone

Solo Traveller Mode

Enable solo mode for check-in reminders, emergency contact prompts, and safety tips tailored to travelling alone.

Solo Traveller Mode
Share your itinerary
Always leave a copy of your full itinerary (hotels, dates, flights) with someone at home. Update them on any changes.
Enable location sharing
Share your phone's live location with a trusted contact. Google Maps, Apple Find My, or WhatsApp location sharing are easy options.
Secure your documents
Keep digital copies of passport, visa, and insurance in encrypted cloud storage. Email copies to yourself and your emergency contact.
Stay socially connected
Join hostel activities or tours to meet other travellers. Solo doesn't mean isolated — communities make travel safer and richer.
Know your exit
In every new location, identify the nearest exit, note your hotel address in the local language, and know how to reach your embassy.
Trust your instincts
If a situation, person, or place feels wrong — leave. Your instinct is a survival tool. Never feel you have to explain yourself.

Health & wellbeing

Medical Assistance

Vaccination requirements and recommendations for every destination, plus essential reminders about travel insurance and health precautions.

Travel Insurance
Always get comprehensive cover including medical evacuation. Check it covers your planned activities (skiing, diving, etc.).
Travel Clinic Visit
Book a travel health consultation at least 6–8 weeks before departure to allow time for vaccinations to take effect.
NHS Fit for Travel
The NHS Fit for Travel website provides authoritative vaccination advice for all countries. Use it alongside this guide.
GHIC / EHIC Card
Your Global Health Insurance Card covers emergency NHS-equivalent care in EU countries and some others. Always carry it.
