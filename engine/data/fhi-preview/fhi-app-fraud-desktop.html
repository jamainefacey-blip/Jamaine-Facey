APP Fraud — Can You Get Your Money Back?

Yes. Under UK law, your bank is legally required to reimburse most APP fraud losses up to £85,000. Here's exactly what to do.

The short answer

Under the Payment Systems Regulator (PSR) mandatory reimbursement rules — effective 7 October 2024 — UK banks must reimburse victims of Authorised Push Payment (APP) fraud up to £85,000 per claim. Call your bank immediately and use the words "APP fraud".

What to do right now

Every minute counts. Act immediately to maximise your chance of recovery.

  1. 1
    Call your bank now

    Call the number on the back of your card. Tell them you've been a victim of APP fraud. Ask for the Payment Services or Fraud team.

  2. 2
    Say "APP fraud" — it triggers a legal protocol

    Under PSR rules, the words "Authorised Push Payment fraud" activate a mandatory investigation process. Banks are legally required to reimburse most losses up to £85,000.

  3. 3
    Request the receiving bank is contacted

    Your bank can contact the receiving bank to freeze the funds. Time is critical — the sooner you call, the higher the chance of recovery.

  4. 4
    Report to Action Fraud

    Report online at actionfraud.police.uk or call 0300 123 2040. You'll get a crime reference number — keep it.

  5. 5
    If refused, escalate to the Financial Ombudsman

    If your bank refuses to reimburse or delays unreasonably, escalate to the Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) for free. FOS decisions are binding on your bank.

Am I eligible for reimbursement?

PSR rules cover most APP fraud victims. Check the criteria below.

  • ✓You authorised the payment yourself (you transferred the money)
  • ✓You were deceived into making the transfer — e.g. fake invoice, impersonation, romance scam
  • ✓Payment was made from a UK bank account to another UK account
  • ✓The fraud occurred on or after 7 October 2024
  • ✗You made the payment knowingly to commit a fraud yourself
  • ✗You were grossly negligent (ignored clear warnings from your bank)
  • ✗International transfers — PSR rules apply to UK domestic payments only

Not sure? Contact the Financial Ombudsman Service — their assessment is free and they can advise before you make a formal complaint.

Limitations and exceptions

The PSR scheme covers most victims — but there are exceptions to be aware of.

£85,000 reimbursement cap

The maximum mandatory reimbursement is £85,000 per claim. Losses above this are not covered by the mandatory scheme.

13-month reporting window

You must report the fraud to your bank within 13 months of the final payment. After this window, mandatory reimbursement does not apply.

Gross negligence exception

If you ignored explicit, specific warnings from your bank about the payment, your bank may reduce or refuse reimbursement.

Voluntary payments for illegal goods

Payments made knowingly to purchase illegal goods or services are excluded, even if you were subsequently defrauded.
